--- layout: roulette-system title: Itza Couple System - Roulette System Review ---

This is a {% include roulette-system-type.html type="Multi-Leg" %} system with both legs being Fibonacci Ladders. When you lose, increase in the Fibonacci sequence.

Bet $10 each on two dozens. If one hits, pull go back down 2 levels for that bet. If it misses, increase by one level in the Fibonacci sequence.

Since this is a Multi-Leg (mostly) Negative Progresion system, I'm already skeptical that it will do well. You just have too many single points of failure for this to be a reliable system. Most of the time one or the other leg will take you out.

If you MUST play this system, I'd at least bet 1 leg as a dozen and the other as a column. That way it's possible for both bets to win, and winning one bet guarantee the loss of the other. Having that correlation is just horrible when you're trying to avoid an unlikely event.

As for "playing as a couple", that shit is for the birds. If you're pooling your wins and losses, you have to look at all the bets as a whole. That means if you're betting on 3 different dozens, then you're betting on 36 or more numbers at a time.

That's a no-no.
