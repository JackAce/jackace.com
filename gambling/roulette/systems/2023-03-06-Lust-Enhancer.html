Betting system where you move the even money bets around and {% include roulette-system-type.html type="Martingale" %} when you lose.

Betting pattern:

  • Low (1-18)
  • Black
  • Even
  • High (19-36)
  • Red
  • Odd

Keeping track of the even money bets is a waste of time, just pick one and be done with it.

I'm only providing a betting graphic for the first tier, because they're all just doubled up Martingale even money bets.

If you just picked an Even money bet or if you just picked a random one, I'd give this system a {% include ceg-system-rating.html rating="c" showRatingLetter=true %} or a {% include ceg-system-rating.html rating="b" showRatingLetter=true -%}.

In the video, Alex asked me to calculate the probability of going bust using this system.

So here's my response:

With this system, you have 212 units... which is bizarre for a Martingale system.
You can only lose 7 in a row. You won't be able to make an 8th bet.
Losing 7 in a row, means you'll be down 127 units or $1270. You won't have enough money to bet $1280 after that.

So, losing 7 in a row, the probability is \( {\frac{20}{38}} ^7 \) which is 0.0112 or 1.12% or 1 out of every 89 attempts.

It makes absolutely no difference that you're moving the bet around. It's statistically the same as if you just bet Black.
