This is supposed to be a derivation of the classic {% include roulette-system-link.html reviewUrl="2020-04-07-24+8" -%}, originally reviewed by CEG in 2020(!).

This is a {% include roulette-system-type.html type="Positive Progression" %} system where you start out with a $60 bet on a Dozen, and $5 each on 12 numbers Straight-Up in another Dozen. In reality, they don't need to be in the other dozen. You can just sprinkle them around the felt where ever you want, so long as they don't overlap with the Dozen bet.

When you hit, remove one of the Straight-Up bets. Keep removing them until you only have 4 Straight-Up bets left.

When you lose, you D'Alembert in place according to your last bet. So if yo were at the $60 + 6x$5 level when you missed, then your next bet would be $120 + 6x$10 for the next bet. If that loses again, your next bet would be $180 + 6x$15.

I'm not going to provide images for the D'Alembert increases. There are just too many permutations to cover every scenario.
