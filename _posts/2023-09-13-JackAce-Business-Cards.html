Why are some Decisions INCORRECT???

Since the decisions are simplified, that means that you're not always making the optimal play. Instead of remembering that you hit a Hard 12 versus a dealer 2 and 3, the simplified set of rules says you ALWAYS stand on a stiff hand versus a dealer's low card (2-6).

By doing this, we've reduced 50 decisions down to just 2 simple rules.

And of the 50 decisions, there are only 2 incorrect plays, which each have very small changes to the overall expected house advantage. You can watch this video for more details, but the short version of the story is that you sacrifice a few really close plays in exchange for having to learn some of the finer points of Basic Strategy.
