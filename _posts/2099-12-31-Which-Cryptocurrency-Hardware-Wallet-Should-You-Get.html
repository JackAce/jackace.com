What is a Hardware Wallet?

When you hear the term "wallet", you usually think of an object that holds valuables inside. It's true that a hardware wallet protects valuables, but it's not the cryptocurrencies themselves. A crypto wallet doesn't hold crypto. The valuable thing you're holding is the "keys".

I'll say it again:

A crypto wallet doesn't hold crypto.

A cryptocurrency hardware wallet is just a device that manages your access (private keys) to coins you have stored in the global blockchain. When you want to move coins from one address to another, the hardware wallet requests the transaction and stamps it with a signature that only it know how to create.
